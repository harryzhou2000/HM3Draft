    \begin{abstract}  
        A family of new implicit time marching schemes based on 
        temporally Direct Integration and Temporal 
        Reconstruction (DITR) are developed and tested in this paper.
        These schemes can be used to solve ordinary differential equations (ODEs)
        directly or solve ODEs arising from semi-discrete schemes
         solving partial differential equations (PDEs).
         he most significant advantages of these methods are that temporally third- and fourth-order
          schemes can be constructed straightforwardly, 
          and require fewer stages than some popular implicit Runge-Kutta schemes. 
        As a ODE solver, the DITR method are found to be 
        $A$-stable, with two variants of them being $L$-stable. 
        When being extended to solve compressible Euler and Navier-Stokes equations that are spatially discretized using  high-order compact finite volumes, 
        the challenge is the design of an efficient and stable iteration method. 
        To this end, a stage-decoupled iteration scheme is proposed and tested. 
        This iteration scheme is implemented together with the dual time-stepping 
        technique and the matrix-free LU-SGS method. Numerical results show that 
        DITR methods achieve high order of accuracy with comparatively 
        lower computational cost. When reaching the same error, 
        some DITR methods are able to save significant amount of time compared with the implicit ESDIRK4 method.
    \end{abstract}
